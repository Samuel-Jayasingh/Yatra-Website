Rules and Regulations

  • Each team must have the required number of players. A substitute can be added before the tournament. Players can only play for one team.
  • No abusive behavior, bad language, cheating, or hacking. Breaking these rules will lead to disqualification.
  • Players must join 15 minutes before the match starts. Late entries are not allowed.
  • Only mobile devices are allowed for games like BGMI and Free Fire. No emulators, iPads, or tablets.
  • Some games require downloading specific maps. In BGMI, teams must share match results after playing.
  • Since this is an inter-college event, streaming on social media is not allowed.
  • If scores are tied, the winner is decided based on total kills or victories (e.g., Chicken Dinners in BGMI or Booyah in Free Fire).
  • Players must meet the required ID level (e.g., 50+ for BGMI, 45+ for Free Fire).
